Research

The explosive growth of data, which we have been witnessing since the onset of the new century, has greatly reshaped our everyday life. From oil wells to shopping malls, from New York to Hong Kong, massive amount of data is being acquired every hour, and thus analyzing large data sets (often called big data) has become the most important challenge that we face as computer scientists. In alignment with this trend, my research centers on the development of probabilistic models and inference techniques to analyze large-scale data, especially those arising from computer vision, text mining, and related areas.

Below is a selected list of research projects that I have done in recent year, each with a brief description.

Interests

Selected Projects

  • Bayesian Nonparametric Analysis of Big Data

    Description

    Bayesian nonparametric analysis is an promising approach to analyzing large scale and dynamically changing data as it allows models to grow with data. Traditional methods for nonparametric analysis are very expensive in computation. The primary goal of this work is to tackle this difficulty, that is, to develop scalable inference algorithms to estimate Bayesian nonparametric models over Big data. I derived a new algorithm to estimate Bayesian nonparametric mixture models based on sequential variational approximation. This algorithm can reliably estimate a DP mixture model over streaming data in a single pass with limited memory footprint, making it particularly suited for applications with massive data.

    Relevant Publications

    • Dahua Lin. Online Learning of Nonparametric Mixture Models via Sequential Variational Approximation. NIPS, 2013. [pdf]

  • 3D Scene Understanding

    Description

    The goal of this project is to develop a technique to parse 3D visual scenes. In particular, I developed an MRF model that integrates cuboid detection and 3D object recognition. The model effectively combines appearance, geometric properties, spatial and semantic relations between objects, and other visual cues to provide a holistic interpretation of 3D scenes. The framework is designed to handle large 3D datasets, and has been tested on new RGBD datasets at Terabyte scale.

    Relevant Publications

    • Dahua Lin, Sanja Fidler, Raquel Urtasun. Holistic Scene Understanding for 3D Object Detection with RGBD Cameras. ICCV, 2013. [pdf]
    • Chen Kong, Dahua Lin, Mohit Bansal, Raquel Urtasun, Sanja Fidler. What are you talking about? Text-to-Image Co-reference. CVPR, 2014. [pdf]

  • Probabilistic Scene Model

    Description

    Traditional scene understanding models rely on simplified assumptions to provide tractable description of natural scenes and thus are limited in the expressive power. This project aims at developing new techniques that can leverage the availability of large scale datasets to move beyond such limits. Along this line of research, I developed a generative model called spatial topic processes to describe the layouts of outdoor scenes, which is able to capture spatial dependencies across different parts of the images while allowing substantial variations in the layouts. There are ongoing efforts to continue the exploration of efficient methods to analyze large scale vision data.

    Relevant Publications

    • Dahua Lin, Jianxiong Xiao. Characterizing Layouts of Outdoor Scenes Using Spatial Topic Processes. ICCV, 2013. [pdf]

  • Dependent Nonparametric Bayesian Model

    Description

    Mixture models are widely employed to describe complex data. This work is motivated by the need of analyzing large data sets (e.g. videos and web data) that are dynamically changing, which requires a formulation that allows mixture models to evolve to over time. In this work, I developed a new construction of nonparametric mixture models by exploiting the intrinsic connections between Poisson and Dirichlet processes. This construction provides principled operations to combine and transform Dirichlet processes without compromising the mathematical properties that enable efficient inference. I also derived a dynamic nonparametric mixture model that allows both the number of components and their parameters to evolve over time. This is an important step that pushes the frontier of Bayesian nonparametrics, extending its application to dynamic data analysis.

    Relevant Publications

    • Dahua Lin, John Fisher. Coupling Nonparametric Mixtures via Latent Dirichlet Processes. NIPS, 2012. [pdf]
    • Dahua Lin, John Fisher. Coupled Dirichlet Processes: Beyond HDP. BNPM, 2012. [pdf]
    • Dahua Lin, Eric Grimson, John Fisher. Construction of Dependent Dirichlet Processes based on Poisson Processes. NIPS, 2010. [pdf]

  • Hierarchical Bridging Markov Chain

    Description

    We develop an efficient method for sampling from combinatorial spaces, which uses bridging states to facilitate the communication between different parts of the space. Such states can be created dynamically, providing more flexibility than methods relying on specific space structures to design jump proposals. Theoretical analysis yields bounds on mixing rates. Empirical analysis demonstrates the practical utility in solving large combinatorial problems with complex constraints, e.g analyzing of casual relations in data.

    Relevant Publications

    • Dahua Lin, John Fisher. Efficient Sampling from Combinatorial Space via Bridging. AISTATS, 2012. [pdf]

  • Manifold-Guided Markov Random Field

    Description

    This is a new generative image model that integrates a probabilistic manifold model to express local texture patterns and an MRF on top of that to enforce coherence across local patches. Contrasted with previous methods that rely on a single MRF, this method infers an approximate posterior distribution of MRFs, and combine their predictions in a Bayesian fashion, thus resulting in much higher resilience against noise.

    Relevant Publications

    • Dahua Lin, John Fisher. Manifold Guided Composite of Markov Random Fields for Image Modeling. CVPR, 2012. [pdf]
    • Dahua Lin, John Fisher. Low Level Vision via Switchable Markov Random Fields. CVPR, 2012. [pdf]

  • Cross-Domain Photo Annotation

    Description

    This is a framework for vision-assisted tagging of personal photo collections using context. At its heart is a probabilistic model of context that couples multiple domains (specifically people, events, and locations) through a set of cross-domain relations. Each relation models how likely the instances in two domains are to co-occur. We also derived a variational EM algorithm that simultaneously estimates the cross-domain relations and infers the unknown tags in a semi-supervised manner. Experiments showed significant performance improvements across all domains.

    Relevant Publications

    • Dahua Lin, Ashish Kapoor, Gang Hua, Simon Baker. Joint People, Event, and Location Recognition in Personal Photo Collections Using Cross-Domain Context. ECCV, 2010. [pdf]

  • Geometric Flows to Characterize Motion Patterns

    Description

    It is nontrivial problem to derive a coherent interpretation of motion observed in dynamic scenes. In this project, we introduced the notion of geometric flow to motion modeling, which provides a high level formulation that is able to capture common motion patterns over both space and time. We also derived a Lie algebraic representation, which greatly simplifies probabilistic modeling of flows by mapping flows to a vector space, and further formulated a stochastic flow model and apply it to analyze motion in real world videos.

    Relevant Publications

    • Dahua Lin, Eric Grimson, John Fisher. Modeling and Estimating Persistent Motion with Geometric Flows. CVPR, 2010. [pdf]
    • Dahua Lin, Eric Grimson, John Fisher. Learning Visual Flows: A Lie Algebraic Approach. CVPR, 2009. [pdf]
    • Donglai Wei, Dahua Lin, John Fisher. Learning Deformations with Parallel Transport. ECCV, 2012. [pdf]
